Narrative

Since I can remember, I have been a curious and inqusitive person. It wasn't until I attended a web development info session that I realized it was the perfect career choice for me in which I could build, create, and continue to learn and improve everyday. The web industry is dynamic and ever-changing. I would like to tell my journey in this narrative section in order to illustrate how my path has lead me to become a developer.

My first memory of interacting with technology in a curious way was around the age of 11. My brother Andre and I would meticulously take apart his XBOX, put it back together, cross our fingers and see if it worked. We never broke it, and to this day I remmeber the sense of accomplishment I felt every time the green light came back on after our explorations. I have continued to enjoy taking things apart in order to decipher how something is pieced together to make it function. I am usually the person tasked with troubleshooting family memeber's broken tech. I believe this is because I am not easily frustrated or discouraged.

My first interaction with web technology was at about 14 years old. I was disatisfied with the layout of my Myspace page and wanted to customize it. The pre-made layouts and templates available to me through the Graphical User Interface did not give me the power to alter the page the way I wanted. I began to teach myself basic HTML and CSS so that I could alter the page to my heart's content. I remember breaking things and feeling all the more eager to fix them. I was continually hungry to learn more about the way the webpage worked and how I could change it. I seek to create solutions if the available options do not suit me.

My freshman year at the Savannah College of Art and Design in Savannah, GA my major was architecture. I have always been intrigued to learn how things are put together, how things work, I think that is why I was drawn to architecture at first. I eventually switched majors. I didn't feel completely satisfied with the technologies I was learning. I knew I wanted to build and create, but it no longer felt like that had to take the form of physical structures. I have always enjoyed building and creating things, and I know had the understanding that the digital realm was a part of this.

Objective

I told my story to give context and background about the characteristics I naturally posess, which contribute to an effective and enthusiastic web developer. I am curioius, eager, and determined in my work. I enjoy learning. I truly believe the mind is a limitless instrument that we need to fully exercise and strengthen in order to reach our absolute potential. I enjoy this type of work, and I truly enjoy creating and building things, and watching how they are pieced together. I face challenges head-on and with authentic interest, because I know I learn best from hands-on experience. I am exceptionally creative because of my time at SCAD, and natural interests in photography and design. I am a highly detailed person, I pride myself on doing great work, and I have experience working efficiently as part of a team.

Thank you for taking the time to learn more about me. Feel free to reach out if you have further questions, or require supplemental information. References available upon request.

Course Descriptions & Workshops

Seattle University- Web Applications and Technology

Foundations of the Web

This course empowers future developers with the attitude and abilities to shape and control that tool, ensuring that through their service the web continues to serve everyone. It is intended to provide a broad base of understanding for concepts and techniques that will come up over and over during the process of building for the web, working on web production teams, and taking future courses in web development.

Introduction to Web Development

This course introduces students to the basic principles of web Development beginning with an introduction to HTML and CSS. Students will learn how to create static pages and how to implement designs consistent with web Standards and best practices. Students will also learn about tools to give them feedback on the quality of their HTML and CSS in order to insure accessibility and accuracy. Finally, students will practice techniques to insure consistency of presentation across different client browsers.

Introduction to Javascript Programming for the Web

In this course, I learned how to write JavaScript and how to use it on web sites to make engaging, interactive pages. This course will stretch your skills in terms of logical thought, observation, and understanding how the pieces of a web page go together.This course introduces you to the basics of JavaScript and its use in building websites. You will learn about the basic principles of programming, and apply them in JavaScript projects.

Introduction to Servers and Hosting

This course gave me a useful overview of the ways websites are hosted on web servers and delivered to users. I was able to focus on how to use the tools that are commonly available and provided by various hosting companies. From this course, I gained a foundational ability to use command-line Unix, to modify and operate different deployments. I also learned common command-line utilities, worked with command-line text editors, and moved files around without the use of graphical user interfaces.

Building Javascript Applicaitons for the Web

In this course, I studied how modern web applications are built using HTML, CSS and Javascript. I used contemporary development tools including Node.js, Yeoman, Bower, and Grunt to build an AngularJS application that leveraged a third party API. See (NFLDocket App) I also explored the world of local development, file pre-processors, and a number of modern approaches to CSS including SCSS/SASS.

User Science and Testing

In this course I studied at the various ways of how users are interact with my websites and applications, and what I can do to enhance my sites and applications in light of those discoveries. I analyzed how users use my web pages, which was crucial to building effective sites that engage users and fulfill their requirements. Throughout this course, I published my tests, findings and analytics regarding usability and accessibility on my User Science Journal which is a Wordpress based site.
